What Kind of Games Can Be Built with Small Models (10B Active Parameters)?

## TL;DR
Small models (10B active parameters) can be used to build a wide range of creative and complex games.

## What is a Small Model?
A small model is a type of deep learning model with relatively few parameters, typically used for training on specific domains or tasks. Active parameters refer to the number of parameters that actually participate in the computation. The advantages of small models include high computational efficiency and low memory usage, making them suitable for devices or platforms with limited resources.

## Why is it Important?
Small models can be used to build creative and complex games without requiring massive computational resources. This means that developers can deploy games on more platforms, such as mobile devices and embedded systems. Additionally, small models enable faster game development and iteration.

## How Does it Work?
The working principle of small models is to train a neural network with relatively few parameters to achieve specific tasks or functions. For example, image generation models can use small models to generate images. The following Mermaid diagram illustrates the workflow of small models:

## Differences from Large Models
Large models have more parameters and complex architectures, enabling them to perform more complex tasks and functions. However, large models require more computational resources and memory, limiting their application on platforms with limited resources. ## Conclusion
Small models are suitable for game development on resource-constrained platforms or for rapid development and iteration. Developers can choose between small and large models based on specific needs.
