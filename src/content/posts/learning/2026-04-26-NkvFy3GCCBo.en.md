**The Era's Fracture: The Contradictory Mindset of Young People**

## TL;DR
The era's fracture refers to the two contradictory mindsets that young people face: anti-internalization and lobster anxiety.

## What is it
The era's fracture refers to the two conflicting ideas and emotions that young people experience when facing the pressures and expectations of modern society. On the one hand, young people want to oppose internalization and pursue individual freedom and autonomy; on the other hand, they are troubled by lobster anxiety, feeling that they need to constantly strive and struggle to stand out in society.

## Why is it important
The era's fracture phenomenon reflects the dilemma that young people face in modern society. They face enormous pressure and expectations, needing to constantly learn and adapt to new technologies and knowledge; at the same time, they yearn for freedom and autonomy, hoping to live and work according to their own will. This sense of fracture makes young people feel bewildered and perplexed, unsure of how to balance their own needs with societal expectations.

## Difference from other concepts
The era's fracture phenomenon differs from other concepts, such as adolescent identity and career choices, in its focus and emphasis. The era's fracture focuses on the two contradictory ideas and emotions that young people face, and their impact on personal growth and development.

## Conclusion
The era's fracture phenomenon is relevant to all young people, especially those facing career choices and personal growth and development challenges. By understanding and recognizing this sense of fracture, young people can better balance their own needs with societal expectations and find their own path.
